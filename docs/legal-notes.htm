Legal Notes

Qualified Electrician Requirement (NAV §13)

Only an electricion registered with the VNB (Distribution Network Operator) is alowed to sign off the connection.

Most electricians will refuse to sign off on a DIY-built inverter or BMS because they take on the legal liability for its safety. If your DIY BMS fails and causes a fire, the electrician who certified the system is held responsible. If you DIY the battery, use a reputable, pre-certified inverter like Fronius or Victron. Electricians are much more likely to approve a DIY battery if the grid interface (the inverter) is a known, certified product.

Grid Compliance (VDE-AR-N 4105:2026)

Any inverter connected to the grid must have a Unit Certificate (Einheitenzertifikat) proving it complies with the latest VDR standards:

  • Anti-Islanding: The inverter must prove it will shut down instantly if the grid loses power.
  • Power Quality: It must not pollute the grid with harmonics or DC injection.
  • Getting a single DIY (micro)inverter certified costs thousands of Euros in lab testing. Without this certificate, your network operator can legally order you to disconnect and may fine you.
Registration (Marktstammdatenregister MaStR)

By law, every battery storage system and solar unit must be registered in the Federal Network Agency (BNetzA) database:

  • Deadline: You must register within one month of commissioning.
  • Transparency: This database is public. Ih you register a 15 kWh battery but don't have an electrician's commissioning report (Inbetriebsetzungsprotokoll) on file with your grid operator, it can trigger an automated audit.
Insurance and Liability

Household insurance: Most German insurers (like those following the GDV guidelines) now cover "Plug-in Solar", but full-scale DIY storage is often excluded unless installed by a professional.

Gross negligence: If a DIY battery using non-certified components causes a house file, your insurance company may claim "gross negligence" and refuse to pay for the damage to your home.
