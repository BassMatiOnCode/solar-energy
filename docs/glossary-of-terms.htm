Glossary of Terms

Azimuth

The direction of panel orientation, as compass angles in the northern hemisphere. 0° corresponds to north, 90° is east, 180° means south, 270° is west..

Balance of System BOS

All components (inverters, wiring, mounting, etc.) other than the PV panels itself.

Battery Controller
See BMS
Battery Management System BMS

Ensures health and safety of battery packs. Monitors cell voltages, temperatures and state of charge, balances cells so that they wear out evenly, prevents unsafe conditions such as overheating, overcurrent and short-circuits. Battery status is communicated to inverters or energy-management systems.

Battery Storage

Stores excess solar energy for later use. Technologies include litium-ion, lead-acid, thermal batteries, and flow batteries.

Bifacial Module

A PV panel that generates electricity from both front and back surfaces.

Carbon Payback Time

The time it takes for a system to compensate the carbon emissions from its manufacturing.

Charge Controller

Regulates charging and discharging of batteries in off-grid or hybrid systems.

C-Rate

The ratio between discharge current and battery capacity (IdCb), measured in C or 1/h. In order to avoid damage or degrade the battery, a maximum C-rate should not be exceeded.

Idischarge=Crate×Battery Capacity

The C-rate determines how fast you can safely charge, how much current you can draw from the battery, and how large your battery bank must be for peak loads.

Depth of Discharge DoD

Describes how much of the battery's capacity has been used, expressed as a percentage, where 0% = full and 100% = empty. Some batteries should not be discharged below a certain limit to prevent lifetime shortening (degrading): lead-acid batteries = 50%, lithium-ion batteries 80 to 90%.

Energy Payback Time

The time it takes for a system to generate the energy that was used to produce it.

ESS

Energy Storage System.

Feed-In Tariff FiT

A policy that pays solar energy producers a fixed rade for electric energy fed into the public grid.

Grid-Tied System

A PV system that is connected to the public electricity grid.

Hybrid System

Combines solar panels, batteries and grid connection.

Insolation

The total recieved solar energy received over time, often expressed in kWhm2day.

Inverter

Converts DC electricity (from a solar panel) into AC electricity for household or grid use.

Irradiance

The power of sunlight recieved per unit area, typically measured in W/m2.

Levelized Cost of Energy LCoE

The average cost of producing electrical energy over a system's lifetime.

Maximum Power Point MPP

The point on a panel's voltage-current curve where it produces the most power.

Maximum Power Point Tracking MPPT

A technique used by inverters or charge controllers to keep panels operating at their MPP.

Microinverter

A small inverter attached to a single PV panel.

Monocrystalline Silicon

High-efficiency solar cells made from a single crystal structure. See also polycrystalline and thin-film cells.

Net Metering

A billing mechanism, allowing excess solar energy to be fed into the grid for credit.

Off-Grid System

A standalone system with batteries, not connected to the grid.

Open-Circuit Voltage Voc

The maximum output voltage of a PV panel, when not connected to a load.

Passivated Emitter and Rear Cell PERC

A cell technology that improves efficiency by reducing electron recombination.

Polycrystalline Silicon

Cells made from multiple silicon crystals, cheaper and slightly less efficient than monocrystalline silicon. See also: thin-film cells.

Power Optimizer

A device that "conditions" each panel's output before sending it to a central inverter.

Shading

Obstruction of sunlight by trees, buildings, or othe objects. Shading is a major performance factor.

Short-Circuit Current Isc

The maximum output current of a PV panel when the output terminals are connected (shorted).

Solar Array

A group of solar panels, forming a larger power-producing unit.

Solar Module
See solar panel.
Solar Panel

A collection of solar cells, wired toghether and encapsulated for durability. Also called "solar module".

State of Charge SoC

The "fill-level" of a battery, 0% = empty, 100% = full. See also DoD.

State of Health SoH

Describes the state of the battery.

String Inverter

An inverter connected to multiple panels wired in series.

Thin-Film Solar Cells

Lightweight PV cells made from materials like CdTe or CIGS, flexible and less efficient than monocrystalline or polycrystalline cells.

Tilt Angle

The angle between the panel and the horizontal plane.

Tracker

A system that moves panels so that their orientation follows the sun across the sky.
